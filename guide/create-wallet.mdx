## Understanding Wallets

Medusa Wallet allows you to create multiple Lightning wallets within your account. This helps you organize your funds for different purposes (personal, business, savings, etc.).

<Note>
  When you create an account, a default wallet is automatically created. You can create additional wallets at any time.
</Note>

## Creating a New Wallet

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access New Wallet Screen">
    From the home screen, tap the "+" button or navigate to "Create Wallet".
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Wallet Name">
    Choose a descriptive name for your wallet (e.g., "Personal", "Business", "Savings").

    <Tip>
      Wallet names can be up to a certain character limit. Choose names that help you quickly identify the wallet's purpose.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a Color">
    Choose a color for your wallet card. This helps visually distinguish between wallets on the home screen.

    Available colors include various options to match your preference or organizational system.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create Wallet">
    Tap "Create Wallet" to finalize. Your new wallet will appear on the home screen.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Wallet Features

Each wallet in Medusa includes:

- **Unique Balance**: Each wallet maintains its own separate balance
- **Independent Keys**: Each wallet has its own admin and invoice keys
- **Custom Appearance**: Personalized name and color
- **Transaction History**: Separate payment history per wallet

## Managing Wallets

### Viewing Wallet Details

Tap on any wallet card to view:
- Current balance in sats and fiat equivalent
- Recent transactions
- Wallet settings and options

### Renaming a Wallet

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Wallet Settings">
    Tap on the wallet you want to rename, then navigate to wallet settings.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Edit Name">
    Enter a new name for the wallet.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Confirm the new name. The wallet card will update immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Changing Wallet Color

You can update your wallet's color at any time through the wallet settings menu.

### Deleting a Wallet

<Warning>
  **Before deleting a wallet:**
  - Ensure the wallet balance is zero or transfer funds to another wallet
  - Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone
  - You cannot delete your default wallet while it has a balance
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Wallet Settings">
    Navigate to the settings of the wallet you want to delete.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Delete">
    Choose the delete option. You'll be asked to confirm.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm Deletion">
    Confirm the deletion. The wallet and its history will be removed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Default Wallet

Your default wallet has special features:

- **Lightning Address**: Your default wallet is linked to your Lightning address
- **Primary Receiving**: Payments to your Lightning address go to this wallet
- **Cannot Delete**: The default wallet cannot be deleted while active

<Tip>
  You can set a different wallet as your default wallet in the settings if needed.
</Tip>

## Wallet Organization Tips

### Use Cases for Multiple Wallets

1. **Personal & Business Separation**
   ```
   Wallet 1: "Personal" (blue) - everyday spending
   Wallet 2: "Business" (green) - business income
   ```

2. **Budget Management**
   ```
   Wallet 1: "Bills" (red) - monthly expenses
   Wallet 2: "Entertainment" (purple) - fun money
   Wallet 3: "Savings" (gold) - long-term storage
   ```

3. **Project-Based**
   ```
   Wallet 1: "Project A" (blue)
   Wallet 2: "Project B" (green)
   Wallet 3: "General" (gray)
   ```

### Best Practices

<Tip>
  **Organization Tips**:
  - Use descriptive names that indicate the wallet's purpose
  - Choose distinct colors for quick visual identification
  - Keep your active wallets to a manageable number (3-5 is ideal)
  - Regularly review and consolidate unused wallets
</Tip>

## Viewing Total Balance

Your home screen displays:
- Individual wallet balances
- Combined total balance across all wallets
- Balances in both satoshis and your preferred fiat currency
